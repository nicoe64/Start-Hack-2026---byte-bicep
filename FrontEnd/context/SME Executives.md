# SME Executives

SME leaders who wear multiple hats. They do not have dedicated talent acquisition or innovation teams. They need one tool that solves both talent and innovation access without enterprise-level complexity or cost. Price sensitivity is higher than other company segments.

## Profile

Their narrative arc is Resource Constraint, Discovery, Simple Value, Quick Trial. "We need talent and innovation capacity but can't afford dedicated teams" becomes "CHF 4,900/year is less than one LinkedIn Recruiter license" and "Let me post a topic and see what happens." They need something that works out-of-the-box and delivers value fast.

## Leveling the Playing Field

SMEs often lack strong employer brands to compete with large [[Companies]] for graduates. [[Studyond]] levels the playing field: same access to university talent as Fortune 500 companies. The [[Subscription Tiers|Basic tier]] (CHF 4,900/year) is designed for this segment -- 3 topics per year, dual use for hiring and innovation, no enterprise complexity.

## Key Objections

"We're too small for this" -- the Basic tier is built for SMEs with targeted needs. "We don't have time to manage thesis projects" -- 2-3 hours monthly, with academic [[Supervisors]] handling methodology and day-to-day oversight. "Can we afford this?" -- CHF 4,900/year is less than one LinkedIn Recruiter seat or one consulting engagement.

## Tone

Clear, direct, efficiency-focused. Emphasize simplicity: "post a topic in minutes." Respect budget constraints: always benchmark cost against familiar alternatives. No enterprise jargon. Frame as practical tool, not strategic infrastructure.
