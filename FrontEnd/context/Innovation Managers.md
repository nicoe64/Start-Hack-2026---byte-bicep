# Innovation Managers

The primary buyer for the innovation and R&D use case within [[Companies]]. Innovation managers manage innovation portfolios and need structured academic access without the overhead of full R&D partnerships. They compare [[Studyond]] to consulting firms, university labs, and internal R&D hires.

## Value of Thesis as Exploration Sprint

A thesis collaboration is a low-risk exploration sprint: define the question, get matched with a student and [[Supervisors|supervisor]], receive evidence-based findings in 4-6 months. No hiring, no consultants, no long-term obligation. Academic supervision ensures methodological rigor. The question is not "can they build it?" but "should we build it?"

## Competitive Comparison

Consulting engagements cost CHF 50-150K and deliver recommendations (not evidence). An internal R&D hire costs CHF 100K+ per year with 12+ months to onboard. University lab partnerships are manual and take 12-24 months. [[Studyond]] Pro tier (CHF 18,900/year) delivers evidence-based exploration in 3-6 months per project.

## Narrative Arc

Innovation Bottleneck, Academic Access, Validation, Portfolio Integration. "We need to explore new markets/tech but lack internal R&D capacity" becomes "One failed consulting engagement costs more than an annual subscription" and "Let's integrate thesis projects into our innovation pipeline."

## Key Objection

"Students aren't experienced enough for innovation work" -- for exploration, students bring fresh perspectives, latest research methods, motivated execution, fixed timelines, and academic supervision. Thesis projects validate hypotheses before full development investment.
