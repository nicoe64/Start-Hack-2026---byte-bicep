# International Students

A cross-cutting segment that overlaps with all academic stages. International students face unique challenges: language barriers (especially German-speaking DACH), limited local professional networks, and visa/work permit considerations. They represent a significant segment at institutions like ETH, EPFL, and HSG.

## Unique Challenges

Where local students leverage existing personal and professional networks for thesis topics and career opportunities, international students lack these connections entirely. They are unfamiliar with the DACH company landscape beyond global brands. The thesis period is their best window to build industry connections before graduation. Anxiety compounds: thesis stress plus cultural, language, and visa uncertainty.

## Higher Platform Dependency

International students have a higher dependency on structured platforms like [[Studyond]] because personal networks are thinner. They are more reliant on university-official channels and platforms for discovery. The platform levels the playing field by providing access that local students get through personal connections.

## What They Need

Clear, simple onboarding. Multilingual support. Explicit "how it works" guidance. Language filtering for English-language topics (many topics in tech, engineering, and data science are available in English). Confidence that [[Companies]] are open to non-local students.

## Trust Triggers

- University endorsement signals inclusivity
- Topic language indicators (German vs. English)
- Evidence of international-friendly companies on the platform
- Free access (particularly important for students on tighter budgets)
- Companies on the platform explicitly seeking international talent

## Key Objections

"Are these topics open to international students?" -- yes, companies seek the best academic match regardless of nationality. "I don't speak German well enough" -- filter by language; academic expertise matters more than German proficiency. "Visa/work permit?" -- thesis collaborations do not require a work permit.
