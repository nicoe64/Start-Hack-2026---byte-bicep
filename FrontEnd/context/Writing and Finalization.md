# Writing and Finalization

Stage 5 of the [[Thesis Journey]] (Weeks 16-24): the student pulls everything together into a finished thesis document and submits before the deadline. Time pressure is at its peak and the gap between "research done" and "thesis submitted" is often larger than students expect.

## What Happens Today

Students write the thesis document, incorporate feedback from [[Supervisors]], conduct a final review with the [[Company Partner]] (if applicable), and submit before the deadline. The writing process surfaces gaps in the research, triggers last-minute data gathering, and demands mastery of academic formatting and citation conventions.

## Pain Points

- Writing is harder and more time-consuming than expected.
- Feedback turnaround from [[Supervisors]] is slow, creating bottlenecks at the worst possible time.
- Last-minute formatting, citation management, and consistency checks consume disproportionate effort.
- Anxiety about quality and whether the work meets academic standards.

## Where AI Could Help

- Suggest writing structure based on the research design and [[Methodology]].
- Manage feedback cycles -- remind supervisors, track open questions, surface resolved items.
- Assist with formatting consistency and citation management.
- Identify structural gaps: "Your methodology section doesn't address limitations."
