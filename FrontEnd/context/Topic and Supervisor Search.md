# Topic and Supervisor Search

Stage 2 of the [[Thesis Journey]] (Weeks 2-8): the student has a vague direction and needs to lock in both a specific topic and an academic supervisor. These two searches are deeply intertwined -- the topic shapes who can supervise, and the supervisor shapes the topic's scope.

## What Happens Today

Students cold-email professors to ask about supervision availability, narrow down from broad interest to a specific research question, and negotiate scope with a potential supervisor. If the thesis is industry-linked, they separately search for a [[Company Partner]]. The result is multiple fragmented processes running in parallel with no coordination.

## Pain Points

- Fragmented search channels -- university boards, personal networks, company websites.
- Professors are overloaded and slow to respond.
- No visibility into which [[Companies]] are looking for thesis students.
- Iteration loops between what the student wants, what is feasible, and what is available.

## Where AI Could Help

- Agent-based search across [[Topics]] matching the student's evolving interests.
- [[Supervisors]] matching based on research alignment and availability.
- [[Company Partner]] recommendations that fit the topic area.
- Surfacing hidden connections: "This supervisor has worked with this company before on a similar topic."
