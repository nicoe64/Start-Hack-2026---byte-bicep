# PhD Candidates

Doctoral students with fundamentally different needs from Master's students: 3-4 year timelines, deeper research scope, close faculty relationships, and dual career orientation (academic and industry). Company [[Topics]] serve as research contexts -- data, applied problems -- not just thesis topics.

## How They Differ

Where Master's students need a topic and potential employer, PhD candidates need research partners, data access, and co-publication opportunities. Industry collaboration can be a core part of the dissertation, not a side project. Their motivation centers on publication potential, industry relevance, and post-doctoral career positioning.

## Narrative Arc

Isolation, Discovery, Collaboration, Impact. "My research feels disconnected from industry" becomes "A platform where companies post challenges that overlap with my research domain" and eventually "My research has industry impact, and I have career options beyond academia."

## Industry Connection

The platform connects PhD candidates with [[Companies]] for Innosuisse projects and applied research collaborations. Companies across various industries use [[Studyond]] to find PhD-level talent beyond their local university networks.

## Trust Triggers

- Supervisor endorsement
- Research-grade topic quality (substantive enough for doctoral research)
- Academic control preserved -- PhD candidate and supervisor retain authority over methodology and publication
- Institutional backing via Innosuisse co-development

## Key Objections

"These topics are for Master's students" -- many company challenges are complex enough for doctoral investigation. "Will industry collaboration compromise my academic independence?" -- academic supervision authority is non-negotiable; companies provide context and data, not direction.
