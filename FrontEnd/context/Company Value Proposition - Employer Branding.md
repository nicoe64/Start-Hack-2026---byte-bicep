# Company Value Proposition - Employer Branding

Third value path for [[Companies]]. Year-round visibility across 20+ Swiss [[Universities]], 365 days a year, positioning companies as innovation-forward employers and attracting [[Students]] who align with company values before they hit the general job market.

## Core Value

Company profile and employer branding on the [[Studyond]] platform provide persistent visibility to [[Students]] across all connected [[Universities]]. Unlike career fairs (one day, one campus) or job boards (transactional, commoditized), Studyond embeds employer presence into the thesis discovery workflow -- the moment when students are most engaged with their professional future.

## Why It Matters for SMEs

This value path is especially critical for [[SME Executives|SMEs]] without strong employer brands. Large enterprises attract graduates through name recognition alone. Mid-market and smaller [[Companies]] compete with Fortune 500 employers for the same graduates but lack the brand awareness to get noticed. Studyond levels the playing field by surfacing companies through relevant [[Topics]], not brand size. A 50-person engineering firm with a compelling thesis topic appears alongside a Big 4 consultancy -- and the student chooses based on topic fit, not logo recognition.

## How It Works

- **Company profile:** Dedicated page with company description, culture, and active [[Topics]]
- **Topic-driven discovery:** [[Students]] find companies through thesis topic matching, not employer search
- **Year-round presence:** Unlike events or campaigns, company profiles are always active
- **Cross-university reach:** One profile reaches students across 20+ [[Universities]] and 1,680+ [[Study Programs]]

## Connection to Other Value Paths

Employer branding is a multiplier for both [[Company Value Proposition - Talent|talent acquisition]] and [[Company Value Proposition - Innovation|innovation]]. Companies that are visible and recognizable to [[Students]] receive more and better applications for both thesis collaborations and young talent roles. Branding exposure compounds over academic cycles -- students who see a company during their Bachelor's remember it when choosing a Master's thesis partner.
