# Competitive Landscape

[[Studyond]] occupies a unique position in the market: both highly embedded in universities AND high in ecosystem scope. No existing platform connects thesis students, companies, and professors across universities at scale.

## Market Gap

**99.7% of European companies lack a central system for academic collaboration.** This positions Studyond not as competing for share of an existing market, but as creating a new category.

## Key Competitors

### Handshake

Career marketplace connecting students and companies. High university integration but focused on jobs and internships -- not thesis workflows. Does not serve the professor side or capture thesis output as a hiring signal.

### EURAXESS

EU-backed portal for researcher mobility and career development. Focuses on researcher job mobility, not structured thesis-level collaboration between [[Students]], [[Companies]], and [[Universities]].

### The Forage

Virtual work experience platform providing simulated company experiences. The experiences are artificial and short-term -- not real projects with real business impact over 4-6 months.

## Positioning Framework

| Axis | Low | High |
|------|-----|------|
| **Proximity to University** | Job boards, LinkedIn | [[Studyond]], career services |
| **Ecosystem Scope** | Single-use tools | [[Studyond]], university CRM systems |

Studyond is the only player that is both high-proximity and high-scope.

## Five Structural Advantages

1. **[[Thesis as Work Sample]]** -- turns the academic thesis into a structured hiring signal. No competitor captures thesis output as evidence for talent decisions.
2. **[[Three-Sided Marketplace]]** -- [[Students]], [[Companies]], and [[Universities]] all participate. Network effects that single-sided platforms cannot replicate.
3. **[[Curriculum-Embedded Distribution]]** -- university partnerships provide organic student acquisition at near-zero marginal cost. Competitors must acquire students independently.
4. **Dual value for [[Companies]]** -- talent pipeline and innovation capacity from the same platform, justifying higher engagement and retention.
5. **Academic cycle alignment** -- built around semester timing and [[Thesis Project Lifecycle]] workflows, creating natural engagement rhythms that job boards lack.
