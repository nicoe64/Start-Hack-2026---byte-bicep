# Three-Sided Marketplace

Studyond's foundational model is "a table with 3 chairs" -- a marketplace that simultaneously serves [[Students]], [[Companies]], and [[Universities]] around the thesis process. No competitor connects all three sides in a single platform.

## How the Three Sides Work

Each audience contributes to and benefits from the marketplace in a distinct way:

- **[[Students]]** get career-relevant thesis topics from real companies and access to valuable industry contacts. The product is entirely free for students.
- **[[Experts]]** (company-side) unlock research capacity for their teams and identify high-potential graduates through a try-and-hire model. Companies pay through [[Subscription Tiers]].
- **[[Supervisors]]** (professors) build lasting industry relationships and develop graduates that employers want. Universities participate for free.

## Network Effects

The three-sided structure creates network effects that single-sided platforms cannot replicate:

1. **[[Students]] attract [[Companies]]** -- companies pay to access student talent and research capacity
2. **Study program leaders unlock cohorts** -- one [[Program Directors|program director]] embedding [[Studyond]] into a thesis module unlocks an entire student cohort
3. **Professors deepen the loop** -- [[Supervisors]] use Studyond to source PhD candidates and industry partners, reinforcing institutional adoption
4. **Alumni create a flywheel** -- graduates who used Studyond during their thesis become company-side users, driving organic growth

## Why It Matters

This architecture is [[Studyond]]'s core structural advantage. Pure-software competitors can copy individual features -- a job board, a matching algorithm, a topic database -- but they cannot replicate the interlocking network effects of three audiences with aligned incentives around a single workflow.
