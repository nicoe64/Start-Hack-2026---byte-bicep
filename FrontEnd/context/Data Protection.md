# Data Protection

[[Studyond]] operates under two primary data protection regimes -- GDPR (EU 2016/679) and Swiss FDPIC -- with infrastructure hosted in Switzerland and compliance built into the platform by design.

## Regulatory Framework

### GDPR (EU) 2016/679

The General Data Protection Regulation governs the processing of personal data for individuals within the European Union. As [[Studyond]] serves [[Students]], [[Companies]], and [[Universities]] across the DACH region, GDPR compliance is foundational to platform operations.

### Swiss Federal Data Protection (FDPIC)

The Federal Data Protection and Information Commissioner (FDPIC) oversees data protection in Switzerland. Studyond follows Swiss data breach reporting requirements and FDPIC guidelines.

## Infrastructure

All Studyond infrastructure is hosted in Switzerland, providing a strong data sovereignty foundation for both Swiss and EU data protection compliance.

## Platform Compliance by Design

- **No IT integration required** on the university side -- [[Universities]] can adopt Studyond without touching their IT infrastructure
- **University data policies respected** in all partnership agreements
- **Student data stays on platform** -- [[Universities]] receive aggregate analytics only, not individual student data
- **Data compliance built in** -- privacy controls are architectural, not bolted on

## Alignment with Academic Governance

Data protection is inseparable from [[Academic Governance]]. The governance-first design principle ensures that [[Universities]] maintain control over how their institutional data is used. [[Program Directors]] and [[Supervisors]] are never forced to share data they do not wish to share.

## DACH Expansion Implications

The [[DACH Expansion]] into Germany and Austria introduces additional data protection considerations. Different DPA (Data Processing Agreement) requirements exist across CH, DE, and AT jurisdictions. The DE/AT market entry milestone (Q3 2026) requires updated DPA documentation to meet local requirements while maintaining the platform's compliance-by-design approach.
