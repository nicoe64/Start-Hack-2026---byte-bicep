# Planning Stage

Stage 3 of the [[Thesis Journey]] (Weeks 4-10): the student has a topic and supervisor but needs to structure the work ahead. This stage transforms a research question into an actionable plan -- defining methodology, creating a timeline, aligning expectations, and starting the literature review.

## What Happens Today

Students define their research [[Methodology]] in discussion with [[Supervisors]], create a [[Timeline and Milestones]] plan, begin the [[Literature]] review, and formally register the thesis with their university. If the thesis is industry-linked, they also align expectations with the [[Company Partner]] -- a process that often reveals mismatches between academic requirements and corporate goals.

## Pain Points

- Students consistently underestimate scope, especially the time needed for data collection and writing.
- No templates, benchmarks, or reference timelines for similar projects.
- Company expectations may conflict with academic requirements.
- Supervisor availability is limited, so planning conversations happen infrequently.

## Where AI Could Help

- Generate realistic [[Timeline and Milestones]] based on the thesis deadline, scope, and chosen methodology.
- Suggest [[Methodology]] approaches based on the research question and available [[Data Access]].
- Create a milestone plan that accounts for typical bottlenecks.
- Identify gaps: "You still need [[Interview Partners]]" or "Have you secured [[Data Access]]?"
