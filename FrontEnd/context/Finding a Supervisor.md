# Finding a Supervisor

A universal building block of the [[Thesis Journey]]: securing an academic supervisor who will guide [[Methodology]], provide ongoing feedback, and ultimately grade the thesis. The supervisor relationship is the single most important structural element of any thesis -- it shapes scope, quality, and the student's experience.

## The Problem

Professors are chronically overloaded. They supervise dozens of students while managing teaching, research, and administrative duties. Students face limited availability, slow email responses, and no clear way to know which professors specialize in what or who has capacity. Cold-emailing multiple professors and hoping for a response is the default strategy.

## What a Supervisor Does

- Approves or co-develops the thesis topic.
- Guides the research [[Methodology]].
- Provides feedback at key milestones (see [[Mentor and Feedback]]).
- Ensures academic rigor and standards.
- Grades the final thesis.

This authority is non-negotiable. [[Academic Governance]] means the professor retains full control over methodology approval and evaluation -- AI can suggest and support, but never replace the supervisor's role.
