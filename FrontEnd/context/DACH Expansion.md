# DACH Expansion

[[Studyond]] is expanding from its Swiss home market into the broader DACH region -- Germany and Austria. This is the primary growth vector, building on the model proven in Switzerland.

## Market Landscape

| Market | Universities | Character |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| Switzerland | ~50 (universities + FH) | Small, concentrated, familiar |
| Germany | 400+ (universities + FH + private) | Large, fragmented, regional clusters |
| Austria | ~75 (universities + FH) | Medium, centralized around Vienna, Graz, Innsbruck |

Germany requires a regional strategy (Bavaria, NRW, Baden-Wurttemberg, Berlin) rather than a national one. Austria is more similar to Switzerland in scale and structure.

## Adaptation Requirements

**Accreditation:** Swiss AAQ references need market variants -- Germany has fragmented accreditation (ACQUIN, AQAS, FIBAA, evalag, ZEvA) while Austria has centralized AQ Austria.

**Pricing:** CHF to EUR conversion with local benchmarking. German Mittelstand is more price-sensitive than Swiss companies.

**Trust triggers:** Innosuisse backing and HSG spin-off status need local equivalents. First DE/AT university partnerships are critical for establishing local credibility. International client logos transfer across markets.

**Academic calendar:** German semesters (Oct-Mar, Apr-Sep) differ from Swiss timing -- [[Topics]] posting cycles and student engagement timing must adapt.

**[[Data Protection]]:** Different DPA requirements across CH/DE/AT jurisdictions. DE/AT market entry requires updated DPA documentation.

## Austria Survey

An ongoing survey of 302 study program leaders across Austrian [[Universities]] supports the expansion. Results will inform which programs are most receptive, local pain points, and accreditation pressure points specific to AQ Austria.

## What Transfers vs. What Needs Localization

**Transfers well:** Academic evidence (universal), international client logos, platform metrics, product capabilities, [[Academic Governance]] model.

**Needs localization:** University partner logos, pricing (CHF to EUR), accreditation framing, market sizing, regulatory context.
