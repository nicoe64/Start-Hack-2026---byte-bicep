# Universities

Universities are the third side of the [[Three-Sided Marketplace]]. Their emotional journey follows: Fragmentation, Alignment, Governance, Partnership. The core drivers are industry relevance pressure, accreditation metrics, and the burden of manual partnership management.

## Narrative Arc

"Managing industry partnerships and student thesis topics is manual and fragmented" becomes "What if we could provide structured industry linkage at scale?" then "We keep academic control -- this doesn't threaten our governance" and finally "Let's sign an institutional partnership agreement." University leaders know they need stronger industry connections, but existing approaches (one-off partnerships, career fairs, personal networks) do not scale.

## Trust Triggers

- **Peer universities already using it** -- HSG, BFH, 20+ Swiss universities
- **Governance model clarity** -- the university retains [[Academic Governance]] over student thesis assignments
- **Innosuisse backing** -- co-developed with Switzerland's federal innovation agency
- **Free for universities** -- no cost, no procurement, no IT integration
- **GDPR and Swiss FDPIC compliant** -- [[Data Protection]] by design

## Key Objection

"Do we lose academic control? What's the institutional commitment required?" Universities are governance-first institutions. Any platform touching curricula, student data, or industry partnerships must demonstrate that it enhances (not threatens) academic autonomy. The commitment ask must be minimal and clearly defined.

## Tone

Formal but accessible, governance-aware, partnership-oriented. Respect academic autonomy. Position the platform as enabling, not controlling. Emphasize partnership and mutual benefit, not a vendor-customer dynamic.

## Sub-Segments

- [[Program Directors]] (primary) -- control curricula, unlock entire student cohorts
- [[Deans and Faculty Leadership]] -- approve institutional partnerships
- [[Partnership Managers]] -- manage industry connections operationally
- [[Researchers]] -- need industry partners for applied research
- [[Thesis Supervisors]] -- informal adoption pathway through individual professors

Note: individual faculty may span multiple subgroups. The subgroups describe entry points and primary motivations, not exclusive categories.
