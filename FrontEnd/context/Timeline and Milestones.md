# Timeline and Milestones

A building block of the [[Thesis Journey]]: structuring the thesis into phases with deadlines, deliverables, and progress markers. A realistic timeline is the difference between a smooth process and a last-minute crisis.

## When It Matters

Timeline creation is critical during the [[Planning Stage]], but the timeline governs the entire journey through [[Execution Stage]] and [[Writing and Finalization]]. Milestones typically include: topic confirmation, methodology approval, data collection start and end, first draft, supervisor feedback rounds, and final submission.

## Pain Points

The most common failure mode is underestimation. Students consistently underestimate:

- **[[Data Access]]** -- approvals, NDAs, and data delivery take weeks, not days.
- **[[Interview Partners]]** -- scheduling and transcription are time-intensive.
- **[[Writing and Finalization]]** -- writing the document takes longer than the research itself.
- **Feedback cycles** -- [[Supervisors]] may take 1-3 weeks per feedback round.

Without benchmarks from past theses, students have no calibration for how long things actually take.

## Where AI Could Help

- Generate realistic timelines based on the chosen [[Methodology]], thesis deadline, and scope.
- Suggest checkpoints aligned with common bottlenecks.
- Track progress and send nudges when milestones are at risk.
- Adjust the plan dynamically as the project evolves -- a form of [[Context Accumulation]].
