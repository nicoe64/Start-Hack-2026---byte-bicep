# Execution Stage

Stage 4 of the [[Thesis Journey]] (Weeks 6-20): the longest and most isolating phase, where the student conducts the actual research. This is where plans meet reality -- interviews that fall through, data that doesn't arrive, methodologies that need rethinking.

## What Happens Today

Students conduct interviews, run analyses, and gather data while checking in with [[Supervisors]] roughly every 2-4 weeks (if lucky). They cold-email potential [[Interview Partners]] with low response rates, navigate slow and bureaucratic [[Data Access]] processes, and iterate on [[Methodology]] when initial approaches don't work. Many students feel deeply isolated, especially those writing individual theses.

## Pain Points

- [[Interview Partners]] are hard to find through traditional channels.
- [[Data Access]] approvals are slow, requiring NDAs and institutional sign-off.
- Feedback cycles with [[Supervisors]] are long -- sometimes weeks between responses.
- Students have no reliable way to know if they are on track.
- No peer support structure for individual thesis writers.

## Where AI Could Help

- Match students to [[Interview Partners]] whose expertise aligns with research questions.
- Surface relevant [[Literature]] and emerging research during the work.
- Track progress against [[Timeline and Milestones]] and send nudges when things fall behind.
- Connect students to [[Mentor and Feedback]] resources for specific questions.
- Serve as a sounding board for methodology pivots.
