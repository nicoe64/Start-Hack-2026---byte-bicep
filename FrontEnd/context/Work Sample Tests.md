# Work Sample Tests

The strongest predictor of job performance (r=.54) per the [[Selection Validity Hierarchy]]. Work samples test actual job-relevant skills under realistic conditions over an extended observation window.

## What Makes a Work Sample

A valid work sample requires job-relevant tasks, observability over time, real conditions (not simulated), assessment of multiple competencies, and structured evaluation. A thesis project conducted through [[Studyond]] meets every criterion: the student works on a real company problem (job-relevant), over 4-6 months (observable), with actual company data and stakeholders (real conditions), demonstrating research, communication, autonomy, and project management (multiple competencies), under dual assessment from academic [[Supervisors]] and company mentors (structured evaluation).

## Thesis as Natural Work Sample

The duration advantage is decisive. Traditional hiring relies on 1-2 hours of interviews. A thesis provides 4-6 months of observed work. The signal-to-noise ratio is orders of magnitude higher. This is why the [[Thesis-to-Hire Pipeline]] is structurally superior to CV-based hiring.

| Work Sample Criterion | Thesis Equivalent |
|---|---|
| Job-relevant tasks | Real company problem in student's field |
| Observable over time | 4-6 month duration |
| Real conditions | Actual company data, stakeholders, constraints |
| Multiple competencies | Research, communication, autonomy, project management |
| Structured evaluation | Academic supervisor + company feedback |

## Supporting Research

Students in real-world projects show stronger professional readiness, clearer skill development, and higher placement outcomes (Esangbedo et al., 2024). Thesis and capstone work function as workforce readiness signals (Popli & Singh, 2024). These findings reinforce that [[Thesis as Work Sample]] is not a metaphor but a structurally valid assessment mechanism.
