# Voice and Tone

Studyond speaks differently to each audience while maintaining a consistent brand personality rooted in [[Editorial Minimalism]]. These guidelines ensure voice consistency within each audience's content.

## Students

**Core tone:** Warm, supportive, peer-like. Use "du" form in German.

[[Students]] hear direct, active language. Use "you" and action verbs ("find," "explore," "match") rather than passive constructions. Acknowledge the stress and uncertainty of thesis writing — validate it, never dismiss it. Short, clear sentences. Concrete over abstract. Never condescending or overly promotional. Emphasize student agency: they are in control, not subject to a process.

Avoid: corporate jargon, legal language, pressure tactics.

> **Do:** "Real topics from companies building in your field. Match with a topic in 20 minutes. Free, no strings."

> **Don't:** "Leverage our synergistic platform to optimize your thesis outcome pipeline."

## Companies

**Core tone:** Professional, outcome-driven, ROI-focused.

[[Companies]] expect business language. Quantify where possible — cost per candidate, time-to-hire, qualification rate. Lead with outcomes, not features ("500 qualified student proposals" not "student matching platform"). Concise; respect their time. Professional but not stiff.

Avoid: hand-holding language, oversimplification, vague claims.

> **Do:** "Source qualified talent through live thesis projects. Average cost per candidate: 60% less than traditional recruiting."

> **Don't:** "Our easy-to-use platform helps you find students! Just sign up and we'll take care of everything."

## Universities

**Core tone:** Formal but accessible, institutional, governance-aware.

[[Universities]] require institutional language — governance, accreditation, academic integrity, structured partnerships. Respect academic autonomy; position Studyond as enabling, not controlling. Emphasize partnership and mutual benefit, not a vendor-customer dynamic.

Avoid: sales language, overpromising, simplification of institutional complexity.

> **Do:** "Strengthen curriculum relevance through structured industry partnerships. Academic control remains with faculty."

> **Don't:** "Sign up now and start connecting your students with companies! It's super easy!"

## Cross-Audience Pattern: "Missing Out" Narrative

A recurring messaging pattern across all audiences: frame the status quo cost (what happens *without* [[Studyond]]) before presenting the solution. This creates urgency by making the cost of inaction concrete, then resolves with Studyond's value.

- **[[Students]]:** "What if I pick the wrong topic and waste 6 months?" then "Here are real topics from real companies in your field."
- **[[Companies]]:** "By the time you interview, competitors have already engaged." then "Connect with students during their thesis — 6 months before they hit the job market."
- **[[Universities]]:** "Accreditation asks for industry linkage data, but we're reduced to surveys and anecdotes." then "Every student-company interaction is tracked automatically."

Use sparingly — 1-2 "missing out" moments per page. Always pair with positive resolution. Frame as opportunity cost, not threat.
