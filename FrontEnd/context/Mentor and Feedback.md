# Mentor and Feedback

A building block of the [[Thesis Journey]]: getting ongoing guidance and constructive feedback beyond the formal [[Supervisors|supervisor]] relationship. While the academic supervisor is the primary authority (see [[Academic Governance]]), students benefit enormously from additional perspectives -- peer mentors, industry [[Experts]], and alumni who have been through the process.

## Why It Matters

Thesis writing is often solitary. Students make dozens of decisions -- about scope, methodology, data interpretation, structure -- with limited feedback. The formal supervisor meets them every few weeks at best. Between those meetings, students are on their own, which leads to doubt, stalled progress, and avoidable mistakes.

## Sources of Feedback

- **[[Supervisors]]** -- formal, authoritative, but infrequent. The grading relationship can also inhibit honest questions.
- **Industry [[Experts]]** -- valuable for practical relevance, especially for company-linked theses.
- **Peer mentors** -- other [[Students]] who are at a similar or slightly more advanced stage.
- **Alumni** -- people who completed a similar thesis and can share hindsight.

## Pain Points

- Long feedback cycles: weeks between supervisor responses.
- Isolation during [[Execution Stage]] -- especially for individual thesis writers.
- No structured mechanism for peer support or mentor matching.
- Students often don't know what kind of feedback they need.

## Where AI Could Help

- Facilitate mentor matching based on topic, field, and stage.
- Provide structured feedback prompts ("Here's what to ask your supervisor about your methodology").
- Act as a first-pass sounding board for ideas before escalating to human mentors.
- Enable asynchronous feedback loops through the [[Direct Messaging]] system.
