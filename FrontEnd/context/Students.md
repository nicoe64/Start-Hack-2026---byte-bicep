# Students

Students are the primary audience for the [[Studyond]] [[Challenge Brief]]. Their emotional journey follows a clear arc: Anxiety, Discovery, Confidence, Action. The core driver is fear of picking the wrong thesis topic and wasting months of effort.

## Narrative Arc

The student emotional journey begins with "I'm anxious about picking the wrong thesis topic and wasting months" and moves through discovery ("real thesis topics from real companies exist?"), to confidence ("I can filter, apply, and get matched"), to action ("I should sign up free and explore"). Students are simultaneously managing coursework, career uncertainty, and the pressure to produce something meaningful.

## Trust Triggers

- **University endorsement** -- their own university uses and recommends the platform
- **"Free for students"** -- no cost, no hidden fees, no trial period
- **Proof of topic volume** -- visible [[Topics]] in their specific [[Fields]] (not just a promise)
- **Social proof from peers** -- other students have used it and found relevant topics

## Key Objection

"Is this legit? Is it really free? Will there be topics for MY field?" Students have been burned by platforms that promise and underdeliver. They need to see real topics in their discipline before committing even a signup.

## Tone

Warm, supportive, peer-like. Use "you" language. Acknowledge the stress and uncertainty of thesis writing. Active, action-oriented verbs ("find," "explore," "match"). Short, clear sentences. Never condescending or overly promotional. Emphasize student agency.

## Sub-Segments

- [[Final-Year Students]] (primary) -- beginning thesis, overwhelmed, need curated topics
- [[Early-Stage Students]] -- 1-2 semesters before thesis, exploring options
- [[Mid-Stage Students]] -- thesis in progress, need [[Interview Partners]]
- [[PhD Candidates]] -- longer timelines, research focus, industry partner needs
- [[International Students]] -- language barriers, limited local networks (cross-cutting)
- [[Career-Transitioning Students]] -- finishing studies, entering job market
- [[Lateral-Entry Students]] -- peer/professor referral, need fast orientation
