# Modular Entry

Modular Entry is a design principle for [[Studyond]]: rather than forcing every student through the same linear path, the platform should detect where the student is in their [[Thesis Journey]] and adapt the experience accordingly.

## The Principle

Students arrive at [[Studyond]] at different stages with different needs:

- A [[Final-Year Students|final-year student]] actively searching for a thesis topic needs [[Finding a Topic]] workflows and [[Topics]] browsing
- A [[Mid-Stage Students|mid-stage student]] who already has a topic but needs industry contacts needs [[Interview Partners]] and [[Company Partner]] discovery
- An [[Early-Stage Students|early-stage student]] exploring possible directions needs [[Orientation Stage]] guidance and [[Fields]] exploration
- A [[PhD Candidates|PhD candidate]] looking for industry collaboration has entirely different requirements than an undergraduate

The system should meet students where they are, not where a predetermined flow assumes they should be.

## Why It Matters

A one-size-fits-all onboarding flow creates friction. Students who are deep into their [[Thesis Journey]] do not want to answer orientation questions. Students who are just beginning do not want to be asked about their methodology. Modular Entry reduces time-to-value by presenting the right interface for the right stage.

This principle works hand-in-hand with [[Context Accumulation]]: if the system knows the student's stage and history, it can both start at the right entry point and personalize the experience from there.

## Current Status

Modular Entry is not yet implemented on the [[Studyond]] platform. It is part of the [[Opportunity Space]] and a direction explored in the [[Challenge Brief]]. The current platform treats all student entry points similarly.
