# Opportunity Space

The gaps in Studyond's current AI capabilities — and the territory that hackathon teams can claim. Today's AI handles discovery; the opportunity is everything that comes after.

## What Exists Today

Studyond already has working AI for:

- [[Student AI Agent]] — conversational topic discovery based on student profile and interests
- [[Expert AI Agent]] — helps company experts create and refine thesis topics
- [[Matching Engine]] — algorithmic matching of students to topics by field, level, and interests
- Profile checking — validation that student profiles are complete enough for quality matches

## What Does NOT Exist Yet

### Adaptive Onboarding

No system detects where a student is in their [[Thesis Journey]] and adapts accordingly. [[Modular Entry]] is a design principle but not yet implemented — every student gets the same linear flow regardless of whether they have a topic, a supervisor, both, or neither.

### Planning and Execution Support

No milestone or [[Timeline and Milestones|timeline planning]]. No AI support beyond discovery — nothing for the [[Planning Stage]], [[Execution Stage]], or [[Writing and Finalization]] phases. Students are on their own once they have a topic.

### Context Persistence

No [[Context Accumulation]] across conversations. Each interaction starts from scratch. The platform does not build a richer understanding of the student over time.

### Autonomous Agents

No agents that proactively search on the student's behalf — finding [[Interview Partners]], suggesting [[Literature]], identifying potential [[Company Partner|company partners]], or connecting students with [[Supervisors]] in their field.

### Structured Mentor Access

No system for structured [[Mentor and Feedback|mentor feedback loops]]. No progress tracking or nudges. No peer connections between students working on related topics.

### Cross-Entity Intelligence

No recommendations that span entity types — e.g., "This supervisor's research aligns with this company's topic and this expert's domain."

## Why This Matters

This is where the [[Challenge Brief]] lives. The current platform handles the first step (find a topic) but leaves students unsupported for the remaining 4-6 months of their thesis. See [[Possible Directions]] for suggested approaches to filling these gaps.
