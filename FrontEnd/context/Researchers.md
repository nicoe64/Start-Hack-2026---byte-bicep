# Researchers

Faculty members who need industry partners for applied research, PhD projects, and Innosuisse collaborations. They have research capacity (PhD students, postdocs, methodological expertise) but lack industry connections beyond their personal network.

## Profile

Their narrative arc is Network Limitation, Discovery, Relevance Check, Outreach. "I need industry partners for my PhD candidate's research but lack connections outside my personal network" becomes "These companies are working on problems in my research domain" and "I'll reach out to explore a collaboration." They want relevant partnerships without institutional brokering overhead.

## What They Need

The ability to browse [[Companies]] by domain, connect with [[Experts]], and access applied research projects without brokerage overhead. Direct access to companies working on problems in their research domain. Free for university researchers with no institutional gatekeeping.

## Overlap with Thesis Supervisors

Many professors span both the researcher and [[Thesis Supervisors]] roles simultaneously. A professor who discovers [[Studyond]] as a thesis supervisor (bottom-up, informal adoption) may later use it for research collaboration, Innosuisse projects, or [[PhD Candidates]] placement. Same individuals, different entry points and motivations.

## Trust Triggers

- Research domain relevance -- companies working on problems in their field
- Direct access -- no institutional gatekeeping or lengthy procurement
- Mutual benefit framing -- companies need research expertise, not cheap labor
- Academic freedom preserved -- collaborations do not compromise research autonomy
- Publication opportunities -- potential for joint papers and industry data access

## Key Objections

"Companies want consultants, not research partners" -- companies on Studyond post research problems, not consulting needs. "Will this compromise academic freedom?" -- methodology and publication rights follow standard academic research agreements.
