# Data Access

A building block of the [[Thesis Journey]]: obtaining the data needed to conduct research. Whether quantitative datasets, internal company data, survey access, or API endpoints, data availability often determines what a thesis can realistically accomplish.

## The Problem

Data access is one of the most common bottlenecks during the [[Execution Stage]]. Challenges include slow institutional approvals, NDA requirements, unclear data ownership, privacy restrictions (see [[Data Protection]]), and companies that promise data but deliver late or not at all. Students who cannot secure data in time are forced to change their methodology or narrow their scope mid-project.

## Types of Data

- **Internal company data** -- requires a [[Company Partner]] relationship and often NDAs.
- **Survey data** -- requires respondent recruitment, ethics approval, and time.
- **Public datasets** -- available but may not fit the specific research question.
- **Qualitative data** -- gathered through [[Interview Partners]] and typically requires consent management.

## How a Company Partner Helps

[[Company Partner]] relationships are the primary facilitator of proprietary data access. Companies that post [[Topics]] on [[Studyond]] implicitly signal willingness to share relevant data with thesis students, reducing the friction of cold requests.

## Where AI Could Help

AI could match students to [[Companies]] with relevant data based on the research question, flag data availability early in the [[Planning Stage]], and help students draft data access requests or NDA templates.
