# Skills-Based Hiring

The macro shift away from degree prestige and CV credentials toward demonstrated capability. McKinsey (2021) found that skills-based hiring outperforms pedigree-based hiring, particularly in fast-changing roles.

## The Shift

Traditional hiring relies on institutional prestige (which university?), credential signaling (which degree?), and CV surface quality (how polished?). Skills-based hiring focuses instead on demonstrated competencies: what can a candidate actually do, as observed through real work? This reduces bias, expands talent pools, and produces better hires.

## Why It Matters Now

The case for skills-based evaluation is strengthening from multiple directions. [[CV Signal Degradation]] from GenAI means CVs are becoming less reliable screening tools. The [[Selection Validity Hierarchy]] shows that credentials and CVs have always been weak predictors (.18-.26). Meanwhile, [[Work Sample Tests]] remain the strongest predictor (.54). The convergence of these trends makes skills-based hiring not just preferable but necessary.
