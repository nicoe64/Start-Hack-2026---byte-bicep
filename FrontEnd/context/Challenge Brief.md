# Challenge Brief

The START Hack 2026 [[Studyond]] challenge: design and prototype an AI-powered thesis journey that is modular, adaptive, and accumulates context over time.

## The Problem

Every thesis student goes through roughly the same process, but everyone is at a different point. Some have a topic but no supervisor. Some have a supervisor but no topic. Some have both but need a [[Company Partner]]. Some are writing but need [[Interview Partners]] or [[Data Access]]. Some are just beginning and do not know where to start. Today, students figure this out alone — Googling, emailing professors, asking friends, piecing it together manually.

## What Already Exists

[[Studyond]] has the building blocks: [[Topics]] (thousands from 185+ companies), [[Supervisors]] (professors with research interests and published topics), [[Experts]] (industry professionals who mentor), [[Interview Partners]], AI matching, student profiles, company profiles, [[Application Management]], and [[Direct Messaging]]. The [[Student AI Agent]] handles topic discovery. The [[Matching Engine]] connects students to topics.

## What to Build

Extend the journey from "I'm starting my thesis" to "I'm handing it in." The thesis is a 4-6 month project — the strongest predictor of job performance ([[Thesis as Work Sample]]). It deserves intelligent support throughout, not just at the discovery stage.

The flow must respect [[Modular Entry]]: students arrive at different stages with different needs. It must support [[Context Accumulation]]: every interaction should build a richer understanding. It must honor [[Academic Governance]]: AI suggests, matches, and supports, but academic decisions stay with the professor.

## What Counts

There are no rigid criteria. A beautifully designed prototype with no code is a valid submission. A working agent system with rough UI is valid. A novel conceptual framework with mockups is valid. Quality and creativity matter more than technical depth. See [[Evaluation Criteria]] for what judges will look for, and [[Possible Directions]] for suggested starting points.

## The Emotional Journey

Students start anxious ("What if I pick the wrong topic?"), move through discovery, build confidence, and take action. The solution should address this emotional arc — not just the logistics. See [[Thesis Journey]] for the full stage model.
