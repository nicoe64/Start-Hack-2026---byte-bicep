# Thesis Supervisors

This is the audience persona for professors who supervise theses at universities. For the platform entity representing supervisors in the [[Data Model]], see [[Supervisors]]. Thesis supervisors represent an informal adoption pathway -- professors who discover [[Studyond]] may recommend it to thesis students, use it for research, or suggest it to [[Program Directors]].

## Profile

Professors who want their students to work on industry-relevant topics but are overloaded with supervision requests and find it difficult to source good topics. They may adopt Studyond informally before any institutional partnership exists. Their narrative arc is Student Topic Struggle, Discovery, Quality Check, Informal Recommendation.

## Overlap with Researchers

Many professors are both thesis supervisors and [[Researchers]] simultaneously. A supervisor who sees students succeeding with industry topics may later use Studyond for research collaborations, Innosuisse projects, or [[PhD Candidates]] placement. The subgroups describe entry points and motivations, not exclusive categories. Same individuals, different entry points.

## Adoption Pathway

The bottom-up pathway often leads to institutional partnerships: (1) professor hears about Studyond from a colleague or student, (2) browses topics to assess academic appropriateness, (3) mentions it during thesis kickoff, (4) sees positive student feedback, (5) recommends to [[Program Directors]] for formal curriculum embedding.

## Key Objections

"Are these topics academically rigorous?" -- topics are problem statements, not finished projects; students apply academic methodology while professors supervise as usual. "Will companies interfere with my supervision?" -- no; [[Academic Governance]] is preserved; professors retain authority over methodology, analysis, and grading.

## Tone

Academic, practical, student-outcome-focused. Respect professorial autonomy. Frame as resource, not requirement. Collegial peer-to-peer tone, not vendor-customer.
