# Orientation Stage

Stage 1 of the [[Thesis Journey]] (Weeks 1-4): the student knows they need to write a thesis but has no clear direction. This is the most emotionally overwhelming phase -- too many options, no structure, and the fear of choosing wrong.

## What Happens Today

Students browse university bulletin boards for supervisor-posted topics, Google for inspiration, ask classmates what they are doing, and maybe attend a thesis kickoff session. The entire process is unstructured and anxiety-inducing. Students are simultaneously managing coursework and career anxiety, leaving little bandwidth for thoughtful exploration.

## Pain Points

- No framework for narrowing down from a vast possibility space.
- Limited visibility into what industry [[Topics]] exist.
- Fear of picking the wrong topic and wasting months.
- No way to test whether an idea is feasible before committing.

## Where AI Could Help

- **Profile building through dialogue** -- not a static form, but a conversation that progressively surfaces the student's interests, constraints, and strengths.
- **Direction suggestion** -- surfacing trends, company needs, and supervisor specializations that match the emerging profile.
- **Feasibility signals** -- indicating which directions have available [[Supervisors]], active [[Companies]], or rich [[Literature]].
