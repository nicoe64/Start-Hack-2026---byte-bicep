# Companies

Companies are the paying side of the [[Three-Sided Marketplace]]. Their emotional journey follows: Inefficiency, Opportunity, Trust, Commitment. The core drivers are talent acquisition cost, innovation velocity pressure, and hiring risk.

## Narrative Arc

"Hiring and innovation are expensive and slow" becomes "A thesis project could be a talent evaluation AND innovation channel?" then "This is structured, low-risk, and university-vetted" and finally "Let me explore pricing and book a demo." Companies spend heavily on career fairs, recruiters, and job boards with diminishing returns while innovation teams lack structured access to academic research capacity.

## Trust Triggers

- **Innosuisse backing** -- co-developed with Switzerland's federal innovation agency
- **University partner logos** -- credibility through institutional partnerships
- **Clear ROI framing** -- quantified cost-per-candidate, time-to-value comparisons
- **Case studies with metrics** -- proof from peers (Big 4, Fortune 500 clients)
- **Governance model clarity** -- transparent data handling, IP ownership, compliance

## Common Objections

"We already work with universities" -- [[Studyond]] is infrastructure for what you are already doing: one access point to 20+ universities with structured governance. "Students aren't experienced enough" -- reframe as exploration, not execution; students bring fresh perspectives and academic rigor. "We don't have time to supervise" -- 2-3 hours per month; professors handle the rest.

## Tone

Professional, outcome-driven, ROI-focused. Quantify where possible. Lead with outcomes, not features. Respect their time.
