# Possible Directions

Suggested approaches for hackathon teams tackling the [[Challenge Brief]]. These are starting points, not requirements — teams can combine approaches, extend them, or invent entirely new ones.

## 1. Modular Flow Wizard

Detect where the student is in their [[Thesis Journey]] and adapt the flow accordingly. Have a topic? Skip ahead. Just starting? Begin with exploration. Already writing? Surface [[Interview Partners]] and feedback tools. This directly implements [[Modular Entry]] — the principle that students should never be forced through a linear path that does not match their situation. The wizard builds a dynamic route based on what the student already has and what they still need.

## 2. AI Mentor Chat

A conversational agent that understands the student's thesis context and suggests next steps, relevant [[Topics]], potential [[Supervisors]], and [[Experts]]. The key differentiator: [[Context Accumulation]]. The agent remembers previous conversations, builds a richer profile over time, and becomes more useful with each interaction — not a stateless chatbot that starts fresh every session.

## 3. Thesis Dashboard

A visual workspace that tracks progress, [[Timeline and Milestones|milestones]], deadlines, and connections. Surfaces opportunities proactively: "Your methodology section is due next week — here are three [[Interview Partners]] in your field." Integrates the [[Thesis Project Lifecycle]] stages into a tangible, trackable interface. Could include nudges, reminders, and peer connections.

## 4. Autonomous Agents

AI agents that work on the student's behalf. They proactively find [[Interview Partners]] for research questions, suggest [[Literature]] relevant to the thesis topic, identify [[Company Partner|company partners]] with matching industry expertise, and recommend [[Supervisors]] whose research aligns with the student's direction. The student sets intent; agents execute in the background and surface results.

## 5. Context-Building System

A progressive profile that grows with each interaction. Every conversation, search, application, and preference builds a richer representation of the student's thesis — topic direction, methodology preferences, timeline constraints, skill gaps. This accumulated context drives better recommendations across all platform features, turning fragmented interactions into a coherent knowledge graph.

## Combining Approaches

The strongest solutions will likely blend several directions. A modular wizard that feeds into a persistent dashboard. An AI mentor backed by autonomous agents. A context system that powers all of the above. See [[Opportunity Space]] for the full gap analysis and [[Thesis Journey]] for the stage model these directions should support.
