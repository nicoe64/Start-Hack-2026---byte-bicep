# Enterprise Executives

Strategic buyers at large [[Companies]] who think in terms of talent infrastructure, not individual hires. They need governance, scale, SSO, and compliance. The Enterprise [[Subscription Tiers|tier]] with co-creation modules, strategic visibility, and quarterly outcome reviews serves this segment.

## Profile

Their narrative arc is Strategic Gap, Infrastructure Discovery, Governance Validation, Institutional Adoption. "Our university engagement is fragmented across departments and geographies" becomes "A centralized platform could be strategic infrastructure" and "This has the governance, reporting, and scale we need." They want multi-year talent pipeline planning and visibility across their organization's university engagement.

## Governance Requirements

Enterprise executives are concerned about data security, integration with existing ATS systems, and institutional reputation. They need role-based access control, SSO integration, audit trails, and multi-domain support for independent business units with central visibility. [[Data Protection]] (GDPR and Swiss FDPIC compliance) is table stakes.

## Strategic Framing

Frame [[Studyond]] as institutional infrastructure, not a point tool. University engagement is a strategic capability: talent pipeline, innovation access, brand positioning. Most enterprises have industry connections that are fragmented across departments. Studyond centralizes this into a measurable, scalable, continuous strategic asset.

## Key Objections

"We already have university partnerships" -- fragmented across departments, person-dependent, no central reporting; Studyond provides one platform for governance and continuity. "What's the governance model?" -- role-based access, SSO, audit trails, GDPR compliance.

## Tone

Strategic, institutional, governance-aware. Frame as infrastructure, not tool. Assume organizational complexity. Professional, authoritative, long-term oriented.
