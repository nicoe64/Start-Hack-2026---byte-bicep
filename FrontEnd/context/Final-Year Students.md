# Final-Year Students

The primary student segment for [[Studyond]]. Final-year students are at the very start of their thesis journey, overwhelmed by the choice of topic and unsure how to find one with real-world relevance. They may have a broad field interest but no specific topic.

## Profile

These students are beginning their thesis while simultaneously managing coursework and career uncertainty. The thesis is the final major academic milestone, and the stakes feel high. They are the typical user the [[Challenge Brief]] targets.

## Emotional State

High anxiety, time pressure, and a deep fear of picking the wrong topic and wasting months. They are the most responsive to clear topic volume proof, "free" messaging, and university endorsement. Their emotional journey mirrors the broader [[Students]] arc: Anxiety, Discovery, Confidence, Action.

## What They Need

Curated [[Topics]] matched to their [[Fields]] that they can explore without commitment. They want to see real topics in their discipline before committing even a signup. Entry points include direct links from university thesis module pages, professor recommendations in thesis kickoff sessions, and peer referrals.

## Trust Triggers

- University endorsement -- their own university uses the platform
- "Free for students" -- repeated and confirmed (it sounds too good to be true)
- Proof of topic volume -- visible topics in their specific field
- Social proof from peers -- other students found relevant topics here

## Key Objections

"Is this legit? Is it really free?" and "Will there be topics for MY field?" Students fear a generic platform that delivers nothing relevant to their specific discipline. Show field-specific topic counts, enable browsing before signup, and use field-specific landing pages.
