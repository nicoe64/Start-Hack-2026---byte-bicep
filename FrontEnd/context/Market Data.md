# Market Data

Macro-economic indicators, market sizing, and academic timing data that frame [[Studyond]]'s opportunity.

## Global Scale

50 million students write a thesis annually (Aschari & Berghaus, 2023). 23 million EU companies face unmet innovation challenges. Most of this thesis effort is spent on topics with no real-world relevance. Meanwhile, [[Companies]] spend billions searching for talent and innovation they cannot find. 99.7% of European companies lack a central system for academic collaboration.

## Market Sizing

| Scenario | Spend per Graduate | TAM (Europe) | SAM (W&N Europe) | SOM (2% share) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Low | EUR 2,500 | EUR 12.5B | EUR 1.8B | ~EUR 36M ARR |
| Average | EUR 3,500 | EUR 17.5B | EUR 2.6B | ~EUR 52M ARR |
| High | EUR 4,500 | EUR 22.5B | EUR 3.3B | ~EUR 66M ARR |

Core assumptions: 5 million graduates per year hired in Europe. Innovative companies in Western and Northern Europe with recurring talent demand represent roughly 15%. Conservative 2% market share within 5-7 years.

## Macro Urgency

WEF Future of Jobs 2025: 78 million new job opportunities by 2030, but urgent upskilling needed to prepare workforces. Ongoing vacancy pressure across European labor markets (Eurostat, 2025). The structural gap between academic output and industry needs is widening, not narrowing.

## Academic Timing

Swiss academic calendar creates fixed execution windows for thesis collaborations. ETH Master Thesis runs approximately 26 weeks plus correction window. [[Companies]] must plan topic postings aligned with academic cycles. Each academic year brings new cohorts -- planning now means integrated outcomes by next fall.

## Policy Context

OECD validates institutional collaboration outcomes and the value of structured university-company partnerships. Innosuisse (Swiss Innovation Agency) provides non-dilutive funding for university-industry projects, with decision timelines of 6-8 weeks and project starts within 3 months.
