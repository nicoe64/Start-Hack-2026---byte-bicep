# Deans and Faculty Leadership

Strategic decision-makers who approve institutional partnerships at [[Universities]]. They think in terms of accreditation, institutional reputation, and strategic positioning. They are not hands-on platform users but serve as key approval gatekeepers.

## Profile

Their narrative arc is Strategic Gap, Peer Validation, Governance Clarity, Institutional Approval. "Our accreditation metrics around industry linkage are weak and fragmented" becomes "Other universities have adopted this -- what's their experience?" then "This preserves academic control and aligns with our mission" and finally "Let's approve this as an institutional partnership."

## What They Need

Evidence of peer adoption at universities of similar reputation and scale. A clear [[Academic Governance]] framework showing that academic control is retained. Measurable outcomes for accreditation -- industry linkage metrics, graduate employability data. They evaluate institutional risk versus reward and need to see that the commitment is minimal while benefits are demonstrable.

## Trust Triggers

- Peer universities already using [[Studyond]] (HSG, BFH, 20+ Swiss universities)
- Clear governance model -- academic control retained, no threat to institutional autonomy
- Innosuisse backing -- co-developed with Switzerland's federal innovation agency
- Free for universities -- no financial commitment, no procurement complexity
- [[Data Protection]] -- GDPR and Swiss FDPIC compliant

## Decision Context

Deans approve institutional partnerships within faculty meetings and committee structures. They need materials that can be shared with committees: governance documentation, peer adoption evidence, accreditation impact analysis. The proposal typically comes up from [[Program Directors]] or [[Partnership Managers]] who have seen operational value.

## Tone

Formal, strategic, institutional, governance-aware. Emphasize alignment with academic mission and values. Respect decision-making complexity. Professional, peer-oriented.
