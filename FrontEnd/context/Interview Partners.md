# Interview Partners

A building block of the [[Thesis Journey]]: finding people willing to participate in qualitative research. Many theses -- especially in business, social sciences, and design -- rely on expert interviews as a primary data source. Securing enough quality interview partners is a common bottleneck.

## The Problem

Students traditionally rely on cold outreach: emails to strangers found via LinkedIn, company websites, or personal networks. Response rates are low, scheduling is tedious, and the resulting sample is biased toward whoever happens to reply. Students with weaker networks are at a structural disadvantage.

## When It Matters

Interview partner search becomes critical during the [[Execution Stage]], once the research design is defined and the student knows what questions to ask and who can answer them. Some students begin identifying potential interviewees earlier, during [[Planning Stage]], to ensure feasibility.

## How Studyond Helps

[[Experts]] on [[Studyond]] can indicate their availability for interviews, and students can browse by domain expertise. This transforms interview partner discovery from a cold-outreach game into a structured marketplace. The platform's [[Matching Engine]] could further refine matches based on the student's research topic and methodology.
