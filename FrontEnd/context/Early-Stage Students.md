# Early-Stage Students

Students who are 1-2 semesters away from their thesis but already thinking about it. They browse topics to get a sense of what is possible and start narrowing their interests. Lower urgency but high receptivity to platform discovery.

## Profile

These students are not yet in decision mode. They are curious, proactive, and not yet under pressure. Their narrative arc is Curiosity, Exploration, Reassurance, Bookmark. They want to know that good options will exist when they are ready, and they may not sign up yet.

## What They Need

A sense of volume and relevance -- "there will be good options when I'm ready." Early exposure to [[Topics]] and [[Companies]] builds familiarity with the [[Studyond]] ecosystem so that when the thesis moment arrives, they already know where to go.

## Entry Points

University thesis information pages, professor mentions in earlier coursework, and peer recommendations from older students. The tone should be welcoming, low-pressure, and exploratory: "browse," "explore," "see what's available." Reassure without creating urgency.

## Trust Triggers

- Topic volume and variety across different [[Fields]]
- Recognizable company names posting topics
- University partnership signals
- Free access when they are ready

## Key Objections

"Is this relevant to my field?" (they are exploring broadly) and "Will this still be available when I need it?" (concern about platform longevity). Address with topic diversity messaging and emphasis on growing university partnerships.
