# Thesis Journey

The end-to-end thesis process from "I'm starting" to "I'm submitting," spanning roughly 24 weeks. Every thesis student moves through five stages and draws on a set of universal building blocks, though the order and emphasis vary by field, university, and level.

## Five Stages

The journey unfolds across five sequential (but overlapping) stages:

1. **[[Orientation Stage]]** (Weeks 1-4) -- discovering what to write about.
2. **[[Topic and Supervisor Search]]** (Weeks 2-8) -- locking in a topic, supervisor, and possibly a company partner.
3. **[[Planning Stage]]** (Weeks 4-10) -- structuring methodology, timeline, and expectations.
4. **[[Execution Stage]]** (Weeks 6-20) -- conducting research, gathering data, iterating.
5. **[[Writing and Finalization]]** (Weeks 16-24) -- producing and submitting the thesis document.

## Universal Building Blocks

Regardless of discipline, every thesis eventually requires most of these:

- **[[Finding a Topic]]** -- the research question that anchors everything.
- **[[Finding a Supervisor]]** -- the professor who guides and grades.
- **[[Company Partner]]** -- an organization providing real-world context, data, or problems.
- **[[Interview Partners]]** -- experts willing to participate in qualitative research.
- **[[Data Access]]** -- datasets, survey respondents, or internal company data.
- **[[Methodology]]** -- the research approach (qualitative, quantitative, mixed, design-based).
- **[[Timeline and Milestones]]** -- deadlines, deliverables, and progress markers.
- **[[Literature]]** -- academic papers and references for the literature review.
- **[[Mentor and Feedback]]** -- ongoing guidance beyond the formal supervisor relationship.

## Design Principles

The [[Opportunity Space]] for full-journey AI support rests on three principles: **[[Modular Entry]]** (detect where the student is and adapt), **[[Context Accumulation]]** (every interaction enriches the student's thesis profile), and respect for **[[Academic Governance]]** (AI suggests and supports; the professor approves, guides, and grades).
