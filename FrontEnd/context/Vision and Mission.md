# Vision and Mission

Studyond's vision is to become the central hub for academia-industry collaboration. Its mission is to build the infrastructure layer for knowledge and technology transfer between science and practice.

## Vision

"As GitHub is to development and LinkedIn is to business identity, Studyond will be the central hub where academia-industry collaboration happens."

This positions [[Studyond]] not as a niche tool but as category-defining infrastructure -- the default place where [[Students]], [[Companies]], and [[Universities]] come together around research, thesis projects, and talent.

## Mission

Build the infrastructure layer for knowledge and technology transfer between science and practice. Concretely, this means:

- **For [[Companies]]:** One access point to students across 20+ Swiss universities -- structured hiring pipelines and innovation access, always on
- **For [[Students]]:** One place to find career-relevant thesis topics from real companies, entirely free
- **For [[Universities]]:** One system to embed industry engagement into curricula -- no cost, no procurement, full academic control

## Strategic Advantage: Curriculum-Embedded Distribution

[[Studyond]]'s core competitive moat is [[Curriculum-Embedded Distribution]] -- a growth mechanic where adoption flows through institutional integration rather than individual user acquisition.

The mechanism: one [[Program Directors|program director]] embeds Studyond into a thesis module, and an entire student cohort uses it by default. One professor recommends it to students, and word-of-mouth spreads among colleagues. One company success generates referrals to other departments and geographies.

This creates embedded network effects that are structurally different from consumer marketplaces. [[Universities]] control the embedding -- governance is preserved, not threatened. [[Companies]] get institutional access that persists across cohorts. [[Students]] trust the platform because it comes endorsed by their university.
