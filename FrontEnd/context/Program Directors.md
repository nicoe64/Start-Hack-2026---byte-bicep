# Program Directors

The primary university persona for [[Studyond]]. Program directors control curricula, thesis modules, and student cohorts. One program director embedding Studyond into a thesis module unlocks an entire cohort of students by default. This is the core [[Curriculum-Embedded Distribution]] mechanic.

## Why They Matter

This is the single most powerful adoption lever in the [[Three-Sided Marketplace]]. A single recommendation from a program director delivers an entire student cohort without per-user acquisition cost. They are measured on program quality, industry linkage metrics, and graduate employability -- all outcomes that Studyond strengthens.

## Narrative Arc

Curriculum Pressure, Structured Solution, Integration Validation, Cohort Adoption. "Accreditation requires industry linkage, but connecting students with companies is manual and inconsistent" becomes "A platform that structures industry access at the cohort level" and "This doesn't add overhead -- it reduces it."

## What They Need

Real-world [[Topics]] for students, measurable industry engagement for accreditation, and stronger employability outcomes. They need proof that embedding Studyond strengthens program outcomes without adding administrative burden. Zero IT integration, free for universities, and academic control retained.

## Trust Triggers

- Peer adoption -- other program directors at similar universities using Studyond
- Accreditation alignment -- supports industry linkage metrics
- Zero IT integration -- no procurement, works out-of-the-box
- Free for universities -- no budget ask, no procurement process
- Academic control retained -- directors still approve student-topic matches

## Integration Model

Program director recommends Studyond during thesis module kickoff. Students sign up free and browse [[Topics]] in their [[Fields]]. Students apply, companies review proposals. Matched students complete thesis with company collaboration. Program director receives engagement report for accreditation. Time commitment: 10 minutes to introduce the platform to a cohort.
