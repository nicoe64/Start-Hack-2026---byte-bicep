# Lateral-Entry Students

Students arriving through non-traditional paths: peer-referred, professor-recommended, or discovering the platform mid-thesis. They have high trust from the referral source but low context about [[Studyond]] itself. They need fast orientation.

## Profile

Their narrative arc is Trust, Validation, Exploration, Signup. They trust the referrer (friend, classmate, professor) so they are predisposed to believe the platform is valuable. They just need quick confirmation that it is relevant to them personally. Typical entry points include a direct link from a peer, a professor mention in a thesis kickoff lecture, or a WhatsApp message saying "I found my topic here."

## Fast Orientation Need

These students need to understand the value proposition in 10 seconds. Skip the exploration, get to value immediately. The [[Modular Entry]] design principle exists for exactly this persona -- allowing users to land on the most relevant page and see immediate proof of relevance without a guided tour.

## What They Need

- Immediate field relevance -- [[Topics]] in their discipline visible on the first page
- University branding -- their own university logo present
- Clear value proposition explained fast: "free thesis topics from real [[Companies]]"
- No-friction signup to continue exploring

## Tone

Fast, clear, no-nonsense. Assume they are in a hurry. Lead with the benefit. Do not over-explain -- they trust the referrer, just need to see relevance. "Find your thesis topic. 200+ topics from real companies. Used by your university. Free."
